QuestionGen

A fine-tuned 1.7B model that writes AP Biology multiple-choice questions where every wrong answer is a specific, named student misconception — not a random distractor.

GitHub repo Model on Hugging Face Write-up Try it (Colab)

Why it matters

Good multiple-choice questions need distractors that map to real misconceptions, so a tutoring system can diagnose why a student erred. Generic models produce plausible-but-random wrong answers. This model was trained — via a purpose-built dataset — to make every wrong answer a deliberate, labeled error, and returns it as structured JSON.

How it's evaluated (four dimensions, scored 0–2)

DimensionWhat it checks
Spec adherencevalid JSON: 4 options, one correct, every wrong option tagged
Distractor mappingdoes each wrong answer truly embody its labeled misconception
Task qualitybiology correct: right answer keyed, distractors actually wrong
Consistencyreliable behavior across similar prompts

Genetics is scored objectively by recomputing the Punnett cross; conceptual topics use an LLM judge validated against human labels. Models are compared on held-out prompts.

Example generated items

Real items from the model's dataset — each wrong answer carries the misconception it embodies.

genetics

In a plant, coat color (black, C) is dominant to brown (c), and flower position (axial, F) is dominant to terminal (f). The genes assort independently. A cross is made: CcFf x ccFf. What fraction of the offspring are expected to be brown (coat color) and axial (flower position)?

  • A. 1/2
    map_answers_single_trait — Gives the probability/fraction for one trait when the stem asks for the joint (both-traits, 'AND') outcome.
  • B. 3/8 correct
  • C. 3/16
    gen_wrong_punnett_ratio — Knows the 9:3:3:1 / 3:1 / 1:16 ratios but maps the wrong fraction onto the requested phenotype combination.
  • D. 1/8
    gen_genotype_phenotype_confusion — Reports a genotype ratio when asked for a phenotype ratio (or vice versa).
cellular respiration

In the absence of oxygen, how is a cell able to keep making any ATP?

  • A. Oxidative phosphorylation stops, but glycolysis continues because fermentation regenerates NAD+. correct
  • B. All of respiration, including glycolysis, stops immediately because glycolysis needs oxygen.
    cr_glycolysis_needs_o2 — Assumes glycolysis is aerobic and cannot proceed without O2.
  • C. The cell switches to fermentation, which now directly produces most of its ATP.
    cr_fermentation_makes_atp — Believes fermentation itself generates ATP, rather than regenerating NAD+ so glycolysis can continue producing ATP.
  • D. The cell spends its stored NADH directly as ATP to get by.
    cr_nadh_makes_atp_directly — Treats NADH as a direct ATP product rather than an electron carrier that feeds the ETC.
enzymes

How does an enzyme increase the rate of a chemical reaction?

  • A. It lowers the activation energy required for the reaction to proceed. correct
  • B. It increases the activation-energy barrier of the reaction.
    enz_raise_activation_energy — States enzymes increase activation energy, reversing their role of lowering it.
  • C. It binds and converts any available molecule to force the reaction.
    enz_specificity_any_substrate — Ignores active-site specificity, treating an enzyme as able to catalyze unrelated reactions.
  • D. It shifts the reaction's equilibrium toward the products.
    enz_change_equilibrium — Thinks enzymes shift the reaction equilibrium or change ΔG rather than only speeding the approach to equilibrium.
